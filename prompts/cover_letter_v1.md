# Cover Letter Prompt v1

## Target Persona

Senior engineering leadership (CTO/VP/Director level)

## Tone

- Professional, punchy, "high agency"
- NO fluff, NO generic mission praise
- NO "I'm excited to..." or similar weak openings
- Concrete claims, tight verbs, measurable outcomes

## Structure

- Exactly 1-2 paragraphs in the body (not counting greeting/signature)
- Each paragraph max ~5-7 lines

## Content Rules

### Paragraph 1 (Lead)

Lead with ONE of:

- A win (framing the problem, decomposing intent, correcting systems)
- A major financial unlock ($X savings, $X CapEx, X% cost reduction)

The lead win MUST:

- Be grounded in the candidate's verified experience
- Include at least one quantified metric
- Match a signal from the job description (cost, scale, reliability, data, leadership, partnerships)

### Paragraph 2 (Optional)

- Second verified win mapped to JD requirements
- One line on operating style (decision velocity, feedback loops, hiring bar)

### Close

- Strong professional call to action
- Example: "If you want a leader who can diagnose the system fast, align execution, and ship measurable outcomes, I'd welcome a conversation."

### Sign-off

```
Sincerely,
[Full Name]
```

## Win Selection Algorithm

1. Extract role signals from JD (cost, scale, reliability, data, platform, leadership, partnerships)
2. Score candidate wins against signals
3. Tie-breakers:
   - Financial impact > operational impact > qualitative
   - Recency
   - Scope match (org size, budget, systems scale)
4. Select top win as lead

## Forbidden Phrases

- "I'm excited to..."
- "I believe I would be a great fit..."
- "I am passionate about..."
- "Thank you for considering..."
- Any generic company mission praise without specific product/strategy reference

## Validation Checks

- [ ] 1-2 paragraphs only
- [ ] At least one metric ($, %, multiplier)
- [ ] No forbidden phrases
- [ ] Ends with strong CTA
- [ ] Sign-off present
